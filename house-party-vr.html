House Party VR

Mixed reality DJ presence prototype for Meta Quest 3

Role Solo designer + Unity prototyper
Platform Meta Quest 3 (MR Passthrough)
Timeline 4 weeks (concept → prototype)
Type Social MR experience (prototype)

Overview

House Party is a mixed reality prototype where a DJ appears in your real room and helps you "host the vibe". Instead of mixing tracks, the player controls a few high-impact moments: lighting intensity, reactions, and simple interactions that make the space feel alive.

This project is focused on one thing: testing how far "presence" can go with lightweight mechanics and minimal UI.

The problem

A lot of VR music experiences feel passive. You watch a performance, enjoy it for a few minutes, then leave. I wanted to explore a different angle: give the player ownership over the energy of the room, without turning it into a complicated DJ tool.

What I built

  • MR setup: Passthrough experience with the DJ anchored in the player's physical space
  • DJ presence loop: simple "hosting" moments (start, build, drop, cooldown)
  • Gesture-first interactions: grab, throw, wave, clap (kept intentionally limited)
  • Audio-reactive visuals: lighting and effects respond to tempo and energy changes
  • Iteration workflow: rapid tweaks based on short play sessions and friction notes

Core design choices

Mixed reality first

Placing the DJ in the player's actual room creates instant context and reduces navigation and setup time.

Player as host, not performer

Early on, "player as DJ" felt like work. The host fantasy kept the experience playful and low-pressure.

Minimal UI

Any menu or panel breaks the vibe fast. I tried to keep interactions physical and obvious, with as little interface as possible.

System-driven reactivity

Instead of scripted sequences, I focused on reactive behavior so different songs can still feel fresh.

What I learned

  • Presence often beats feature count
  • Fewer interactions, done well, feel better than many interactions done halfway
  • In MR, friction shows up immediately (setup, UI, unclear affordances)
  • Audio reactivity needs to feel tight or it reads as "random", not "alive"

Next steps

  • Tighten the MVP loop into a repeatable 5–10 minute experience
  • Improve beat/energy detection and reduce latency feel
  • Add 1 "party moment" mini interaction (example: hoop shot triggers a drop/confetti)
  • Polish onboarding: fast start, clear affordances, controller fallback if needed
  • Record a short demo video and publish a playable build

Tech notes

Unity + Meta XR SDK (Passthrough MR, hand tracking). Lightweight real-time audio analysis for reactive lighting and effects.
