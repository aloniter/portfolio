Lucky Tower

A weekly risk-reward event designed to lift mid-week retention (D3-D7) in casual F2P

Role Product Design • Economy Design
Timeline 3 weeks (concept → spec + prototype)
Platform Mobile F2P
Focus Retention event design, session depth, monetization hooks
Play: Live prototype

Screens

Goal

Lift mid-week (D3-D7) retention by creating a high-stakes, recurring engagement anchor.

The Solution

A weekly "push-your-luck" event. Players climb floors for exponential rewards but risk losing everything if they fail to cash out.

What I Delivered

  • Feature spec: rules, progression, rewards, economy, and event configuration
  • Economy model: earn rates, sink design, pricing examples, anti-P2W constraints
  • KPI plan: what to track, how to evaluate, and what "success" means
  • Playable prototype: used for quick playtests before engineering investment

Key Decisions

Weekly event (7 days), not permanent

A time-boxed event creates urgency, supports reward rotation, and stays fresh through theming.

Risk-reward core: higher/lower with "cash out"

Simple to understand in seconds, but still creates tension because every floor asks a clear question: stop now or risk it for more.

Streak bonuses for depth without excluding casual players

Streaks reward smart play, but a single miss does not wipe the whole run. That keeps the feature approachable while still giving skilled players something to chase.

Economy & Monetization (Player-first)

Currency: coins earned by climbing
Sinks:

  • Safety shield (one-time loss protection)
  • Reroll / second chance
  • Cosmetics (sticker pack collection)

Monetization approach: optional convenience. Paying accelerates progress, but free players can still earn assists through play.

Targets & Measurement

Primary targets (for launch):

  • D3-D7 retention lift during event week
  • Participation rate among active players
  • Avg event session length
  • Incremental revenue contribution during the event window

What we would track:

  • Cash-out floor distribution (where players stop and why)
  • Loss points that trigger drop-off (friction hotspots)
  • Coin spend behavior (sink balance)
  • Fairness sentiment (P2W perception)

Data & Insight

Prototype testing showed complex floor types reduced tension. I cut them to focus purely on the 'Cash Out vs. Risk' decision.

Next Iterations

  • Social layer: share "highest floor", weekly leaderboard
  • Theming: seasonal towers + rotating rewards
  • Progression track: optional premium track that adds value without blocking free players
